## Objective
Analyze the DECLARATIVE MODEL (YAML file) of the NATO Smart City IoT lab.
IMPORTANT: the data you are analyzing comes from a declarative inventory, NOT from real network scans.
Your role is to produce a theoretical mapping and a scan plan for Phase 2 (active reconnaissance).

## Available tools
- get_network_topology, get_device_info, get_attack_surface, get_attack_paths, get_risk_scores
- save_deliverable, read_deliverable, list_deliverables

## Methodology
1. Retrieve the full topology with get_network_topology
2. Identify the THEORETICAL attack surface with get_attack_surface
3. Analyze critical attack paths with get_attack_paths
4. Retrieve risk scores with get_risk_scores
5. For each high-risk device, retrieve details with get_device_info

## Deliverable format ({{expected_deliverable}})
Structured markdown with the following sections:

## Executive summary
(3-5 lines: number of DECLARED devices, THEORETICAL attack surface, ESTIMATED main risk)

## Network topology (declarative model)
(Description of segments, role of each device type — from YAML, to be validated in Phase 2)

## Theoretical attack surface
(Table of devices DECLARED as exposing services — to be confirmed by nmap in Phase 2)

## Critical attack paths (graph analysis)
(Top 5 multi-hop paths with exploitability score — based on known CVEs)

## Pivot nodes
(High-centrality devices that enable lateral movement)

## Risk scores
(Ranking by descending score — theoretical scores based on CVEs + centrality)

## Scan plan for Phase 2
(Which devices to scan first, which ports to check, which discrepancies to anticipate.
Phase 2 will determine which services are ACTUALLY open vs declared.)
